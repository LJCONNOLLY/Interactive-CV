Introduction

About

LJ Connolly is a PhD student in Text and Technology at the University of Central Florida, where they work as a Graduate Research Assistant at the Center for Humanities and Digital Research. Their scholarly work sits at the intersection of digital humanities, feminist theory, and critical race studies — exploring how technology mediates identity, memory, and political geography.

Previously at the University of South Florida, LJ earned an MA in Women's, Gender, and Sexuality Studies and served as a researcher on an NSF-funded project examining racial violence and collective memory in the U.S. South. Their creative writing has appeared in The Ekphrastic Review, Bending Genres, and Bullets into Bells.
